WHY CAN'T I OPEN MY SIDELOADED APPS?

• Unable to Verify App

An Internet connection is required to verify trust of the developer "iPhone Distribution: NAME". This app will not be available until verified.

• Unable To Install “NAME"

This app cannot be installed because its integrity could not be verified.

• "NAME" is no longer available

These errors mean that:

• Your device has been blacklisted from the certificate you're using, you will be unable to use that certificate on your device to sign & install apps.

• The certificate you used has been revoked by Apple, and can no longer be used to sign & install apps.

HOW TO FIX?

To fix it (so you can sign, install, & use apps), you must obtain a new certificate.

To do so, choose one of the following options:

• Wait patiently for a new certificate to be released/leaked publicly. Nobody knows when a new certificate will be leaked it can happen in the next 2 seconds or in 3 weeks. You can check status here.

• Purchase an Apple Developer account here (150€/yr).

• Purchase access to a certificate through third-party sites (15.99€/yr).

ABOUT FREE ENTERPRISE CERTIFICATES

Enterprise certificates are shareable certificates that cost starting around $3,000 USD per certificate. These enterprise certificates are usually purchased for use in a large company, and then leaked to users who are willing to pay the price. These certificates are then given to appdb, for free (in exchange for a banner thanking the user at the top of the appdb website), who then makes it available for all appdb users to use it, also for free. However, if Apple realizes unauthorized users are using the certificate Apple sold to the company to sideload non-company-related apps, then they will blacklist the users they detect are not employees. After a period of cat-and-mouse, Apple will revoke the certificate entirely, making it unusable for everyone. The user who paid ~$3,000 for it can no longer use it as well, and there is no warranty provided.

WHEN WILL A NEW FREE CERTIFICATE BE RELEASED?

Due to the high cost to purchase such a certificate and scarcity of enterprise certificates available, there is no set schedule or date/time when these certificates are provided to appdb. They are given to appdb randomly, we can not predict when it will be done. Before you ask when a new certificate will be released, please ask yourself: if you paid for a $3,000USD enterprise certificate, would you be willing to let complete strangers use it to sideload apps and potentially get it revoked by Apple, losing your $3,000? Most people would say “No”, but some generous users might say “Yes”.
